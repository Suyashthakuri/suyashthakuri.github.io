Brokerage Client Revenue & Churn Analysis

Which clients generate 80% of brokerage revenue — and who is about to leave? An end-to-end analysis of 200,000+ transactions to identify high-value segments, churn signals, and deliver a written executive recommendation to the sales team.

47 At-risk clients flagged

The Business Problem

An ASX-listed brokerage manages 500+ active client accounts across equity, ETF, and CFD products. The sales team has no visibility into which clients generate the most revenue, which are showing signs of disengagement, or where to prioritise retention effort.

The head of sales needs answers to three questions:

  • Which 20% of clients generate 80% of brokerage revenue?
  • Which high-value clients are showing churn signals right now?
  • What actions should the sales team take this week?

"We are losing high-value clients and we don't know until they are gone. We need to see the warning signs before it happens — not after." — Head of Sales

Methodology

Step 1 — Data preparation and cleaning (SQL)

Built a client transaction dataset covering 200,000+ trades across 500+ accounts over 24 months. Cleaned and structured the data using SQL — resolving duplicates, standardising product categories, and creating derived fields for analysis.

-- Client revenue segmentation query WITH client_revenue AS ( SELECT client_id, client_name, account_tier, SUM(brokerage_fee) AS total_revenue, COUNT(trade_id) AS trade_count, MAX(trade_date) AS last_trade_date, ROUND( SUM(brokerage_fee) * 100.0 / SUM(SUM(brokerage_fee)) OVER(), 2) AS revenue_share_pct FROM client_transactions WHERE trade_date >= '2023-01-01' GROUP BY client_id, client_name, account_tier ), ranked AS ( SELECT *, RANK() OVER (ORDER BY total_revenue DESC) AS revenue_rank, CASE WHEN total_revenue >= 50000 THEN 'High Value' WHEN total_revenue >= 20000 THEN 'Growth' WHEN total_revenue >= 5000 THEN 'Standard' ELSE 'Low Value' END AS client_segment FROM client_revenue ) SELECT * FROM ranked ORDER BY revenue_rank;

Step 2 — Churn risk scoring (Python)

Built a Python churn risk model using four behavioural signals: days since last trade, AUM change over 90 days, trading frequency decline, and product adoption reduction. Each client received a risk score from 0–100.

Step 3 — Power BI dashboard (4 pages)

Built an executive Power BI dashboard with four pages covering the full client analytics story — from portfolio overview to individual at-risk account detail.

  • Page 1 — Executive Summary: Total revenue, client count by segment, top 10 clients, key risk indicators
  • Page 2 — Client Segments: Pareto analysis, revenue concentration, segment trends over time
  • Page 3 — Revenue Analysis: Product mix, monthly revenue trends, fee per client by tier
  • Page 4 — At-Risk Clients: Churn risk scores, inactivity flags, AUM decline alerts, recommended actions

Key Findings

1

Top 18% of clients generate 80% of brokerage revenue

91 high-value clients out of 512 total accounts drive A$4.1M of the A$5.2M annual brokerage revenue. The bottom 40% of accounts generate less than 3% of revenue combined.

2

47 high-value clients showing active churn signals

47 clients in the top revenue tier show at least two churn signals — 60+ days of inactivity, AUM decline of 20%+, or trading frequency reduction of 50%+. Combined revenue at risk: A$3.2M annually.

3

CFD and margin lending clients churn 2.3x faster than equity clients

Product mix analysis reveals CFD traders have significantly shorter engagement cycles. Clients who hold both equity and CFD products have 40% lower churn rates than single-product clients.

4

Q1 accounts for 34% of annual revenue but only 18% of active accounts

Seasonal concentration risk — Q1 trading activity drives disproportionate revenue. The sales team should focus retention efforts before the Q1 trading period begins.

Executive Recommendation

Sales Team Briefing — Client Retention Priority

Our analysis of 200,000+ client transactions has identified a material revenue risk that requires immediate action.

Situation: 47 high-value clients representing A$3.2M in annual brokerage revenue are showing active churn signals including 60+ days of inactivity and AUM decline exceeding 20%.

Recommendation:

1. Immediate outreach to the top 15 at-risk clients this week — prioritise accounts with AUM decline greater than 30% and no trades in 90+ days.

2. Cross-sell equity products to the 23 single-product CFD clients in the high-value tier — multi-product clients show 40% lower churn.

3. Schedule Q1 retention campaign for January — targeting the 47 flagged clients before the peak trading season.

Protecting these 47 accounts protects 62% of at-risk revenue with targeted effort on less than 10% of the total client base.
