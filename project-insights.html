The Business Problem

An ASX-listed financial services firm is experiencing slowing revenue growth despite a growing customer base. The commercial team suspects that product adoption rates are low and that a significant portion of customers are underengaged — but they have no data to confirm this or to know where to focus.

The Chief Commercial Officer needs answers to:

  • Which customer segments generate the most revenue per customer?
  • Which products have the lowest adoption among high-value customers?
  • Where is the biggest untapped revenue opportunity?
  • What should the commercial team do first?

"We know our revenue growth is slowing but we cannot tell if it is a customer quality problem, a product problem, or an engagement problem. We need the data to tell us." — Chief Commercial Officer

Methodology

Step 1 — Customer segmentation (SQL)

Segmented 150,000+ customers using RFM analysis — Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value — to identify the most commercially valuable customer clusters and their product adoption patterns.

-- RFM segmentation with product adoption overlay WITH rfm_base AS ( SELECT customer_id, DATEDIFF('day', MAX(transaction_date), CURRENT_DATE) AS recency_days, COUNT(DISTINCT transaction_id) AS frequency, SUM(revenue) AS monetary_value FROM customer_transactions GROUP BY customer_id ), rfm_scored AS ( SELECT *, NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY recency_days ASC) AS r_score, NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY frequency DESC) AS f_score, NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY monetary_value DESC) AS m_score FROM rfm_base ), product_adoption AS ( SELECT customer_id, COUNT(DISTINCT product_id) AS products_held, STRING_AGG(product_name, ', ') AS product_list FROM customer_products GROUP BY customer_id ) SELECT r.customer_id, r.r_score, r.f_score, r.m_score, (r.r_score + r.f_score + r.m_score) AS rfm_total, p.products_held, p.product_list, CASE WHEN (r.r_score + r.f_score + r.m_score) >= 12 THEN 'Champions' WHEN (r.r_score + r.f_score + r.m_score) >= 9 THEN 'Loyal' WHEN (r.r_score + r.f_score + r.m_score) >= 6 THEN 'At Risk' ELSE 'Dormant' END AS customer_segment FROM rfm_scored r LEFT JOIN product_adoption p ON r.customer_id = p.customer_id;

Key Findings

1

Multi-product customers generate 3.4x more revenue than single-product customers

Customers holding 3+ products generate an average of A$2,840 per year versus A$830 for single-product customers. Yet 61% of customers hold only one product.

2

Champions segment — 8% of customers — generates 44% of total revenue

The top RFM segment of 12,000 customers drives A$18.2M of the A$41.4M total annual revenue. This segment has the highest product adoption rate at 4.1 products per customer on average.

3

Product B has 23% adoption among Champions but only 4% among Loyal customers

The largest cross-sell opportunity — if Loyal customers adopted Product B at the same rate as Champions, estimated incremental annual revenue would be A$7.4M.

4

At-Risk segment declining 12% quarter-on-quarter — intervention needed now

22,000 customers previously in the Loyal segment have migrated to At-Risk over the past 12 months. Average time from At-Risk to Dormant is 4.2 months without engagement.

Commercial Recommendations

Commercial Team Briefing — Growth Priorities

Analysis of 150,000+ customer records has identified A$7.4M in near-term revenue opportunity across two priority initiatives.

Priority 1 — Product B cross-sell to Loyal segment:

Target the 38,000 Loyal customers who do not hold Product B with a focused cross-sell campaign. Estimated revenue uplift: A$7.4M annually at current Champions adoption rates. This is the single largest revenue opportunity in the portfolio.

Priority 2 — At-Risk segment rescue:

Engage the 22,000 At-Risk customers within the next 60 days before migration to Dormant accelerates. A reactivation campaign targeting this segment's most recent product interactions is recommended. Protecting 30% of this segment at current revenue rates preserves A$2.1M annually.

Priority 3 — Multi-product onboarding:

Introduce a structured second-product recommendation at the 90-day customer mark. Data shows customers who adopt a second product within 90 days have 68% lower 12-month churn rates.

Priority 4 — Champions retention:

Implement a dedicated relationship management program for the 12,000 Champions. Losing 10% of this segment would cost A$1.8M in annual revenue — equivalent to acquiring 2,200 new standard customers.
